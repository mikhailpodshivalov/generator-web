Random workflow

  • Low chaos: preserve groove family and timing clarity.
  • Mid chaos: explore tonal variants while keeping rhythm anchor.
  • High chaos: search broad territory, then reduce density manually.
  • Use slot-level random (Pattern, Sound, FX, P-lock) for focused edits, then global random for full reset.
  • Random FX and random P-lock are independent layers, so you can move tone and motion separately.
  • CC p-lock lanes can drive external MIDI gear, so keep one lane as a stable anchor before heavy rerolls.
  • MIDI Out is already implemented; use low chaos first to keep external modulation readable.
  • After locking a groove, open Song Mode and chain pattern variations into a full timeline.
